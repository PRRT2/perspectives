PRRT2 — Where It All Intersects
PRRT2 (Proline-Rich Transmembrane Protein 2) is an auxiliary subunit concentrated in striatal MSNs and cortical neurons. It modulates Nav1.1, Nav1.2, and Nav1.6 α-subunits, slowing their recovery from inactivation and reducing their surface density. A loss-of-function mutation disrupts this brake: channels recover faster, refractory periods shorten, and the neuron becomes prone to paroxysmal high-frequency bursting.

Because PRRT2 is embedded within the same striatal circuitry that dopamine and GABA modulate, its dysfunction amplifies the instability of both pathways simultaneously — linking motor, sleep/arousal, and sensory symptoms through a single shared mechanism.
