# Basal Ganglia & Sodium Channel Regulation

**An interactive circuit diagram showing how the basal ganglia pathways, dopamine modulation, GABAergic braking, and sodium channel biology intersect — and where PRRT2 sits in the middle of all of it.**

{% hint style="success" %}
**🧭 PRRT2.org Perspective**

_This diagram was developed by the PRRT2 Gene Alliance to explain the circuit mechanisms underlying PRRT2-related conditions. It represents the current mechanistic understanding, including some elements at the emerging-evidence level. Educational only — not diagnostic, not medical advice._
{% endhint %}

The diagram covers four layers of the circuit: the cortical input driving the striatum, the dopaminergic gain-knob from the substantia nigra, the GABAergic thalamic brake from the globus pallidus, and the sodium channel biology that ties it together. PRRT2 sits at the intersection of all three pathways — which is why its loss of function produces such a wide range of symptoms across motor, sensory, and arousal circuits.
