PRRT2 Patient Profile — Nav Channel & Symptom Map

Heterozygous PRRT2 mutation · Haploinsufficiency · Nav channel dysregulation

① Patient Gene Status — PRRT2 Pathogenic Variant
✓
Allele #1 — Working
Produces normal, fully functional PRRT2 protein. Works correctly. Problem: 50% output is not enough to fully brake Nav channels.
✗
Allele #2 — Silent
Frameshift mutation c.649dupC. Extra cytosine inserted → premature stop codon → mRNA destroyed by NMD. Produces zero PRRT2 protein.
② What Healthy PRRT2 Does to Nav Channels
Normal PRRT2 action
Binds Nav α-subunit intracellularly. Slows recovery from inactivation. Reduces surface channel density. Extends refractory period between firings.
My 50% deficit
Channels recover from inactivation faster. More channels at membrane surface. Shorter refractory period. Neurons can fire again sooner — edge of paroxysmal burst.

④ Symptom Constellation — Adult Generalized Presentation
⑤ Each Symptom — Mechanism & Region
Dysphonia
Laryngeal motor neurons receive dysregulated signals from motor cortex via corticobulbar tract. Nav channel instability in laryngeal motor circuit causes inconsistent vocal cord tension and adduction. Most functionally burdensome symptom.
Laryngeal motor cortex → brainstem → vocal cords
Generalized Dystonia
Striatal MSNs misfiring from birth → basal ganglia output to thalamus abnormal → motor cortex receives wrong timing signals → muscles throughout body receive simultaneous conflicting commands. Maladaptive plasticity locked in over decades.
Striatum → GPi → Thalamus → Motor Cortex
Cervical Dystonia
Same basal ganglia circuit dysfunction but specifically affecting cervical motor programs. Neck muscles receive tonically abnormal drive. Years of abnormal signaling has remodeled the muscle tissue around the dysfunction.
Basal ganglia → Cervical motor cortex → C1–C4 muscles
Paroxysmal Episodes
Circuit sitting near tipping threshold due to PRRT2 insufficiency. Emotional triggers → norepinephrine/dopamine surge → Nav channels tip over refractory threshold → sudden synchronous burst. Full consciousness maintained — not seizure. Consistent with partial cataplexy features.
Amygdala → Striatum → Thalamus circuit
Tics
Striato-thalamo-cortical loop instability generating brief unwanted motor commands. PRRT2 expressed in striatal interneurons that normally suppress tic-generating circuits. Reduced braking → breakthrough motor fragments.
Striatum → Supplementary Motor Area
Sleep Dysregulation
PRRT2 expressed in hypothalamic and brainstem circuits governing sleep-wake transitions. Nav channel instability disrupts slow-wave sleep architecture. Possible partial cataplexy during emotional arousal — hypocretin/orexin system stress. Warrants PSG/MSLT evaluation.
Hypothalamus → LC → Brainstem arousal circuits
Atypical Seizures
Nav channel instability lowers seizure threshold across multiple circuit nodes. Seizures present atypically — often without classic tonic-clonic pattern — due to diffuse Nav1.1/Nav1.2 dysfunction rather than focal onset. Present from childhood; frequently misdiagnosed as non-epileptic or functional events.
Cortex → Striatum → Thalamus → Cortex
⑥ What's Happening Inside Right Now
Nav channels
Recovering from inactivation ~2x faster than normal. More channels at membrane surface. Refractory period too short — edge of burst firing continuously.
Striatum
MSNs receiving inadequate PRRT2 braking. Direct and indirect pathways both unstable. Motor output timing inconsistent since childhood.
Electrolytes
Na⁺ deficit weakens electrochemical gradient. Already unstable Nav channels lose driving force. Circuit tipping threshold lowers further on hot work days.
Sleep circuits
Slow-wave sleep likely fragmented. Peak neuronal protein synthesis window disrupted nightly. PRRT2 output from good allele suboptimal without deep sleep.
⑦ Management Approach — Illustrative Example
Targeting the Circuit From Multiple Angles
→
Salt & hydration daily — supports blood volume and autonomic stability. Reduces autonomic trigger threshold for paroxysmal episodes. First-line for any dysautonomic component.
→
Sodium oxybate (off-label) — GHB/GABA-B agonist. Prescribed off-label for dystonia and dysphonia; evidence supports motor circuit stabilization. Also addresses slow-wave sleep disruption and partial cataplexy features. Requires specialist evaluation and prescription.
→
Sulforaphane (sprouts + Jarrow) — Nrf2 activation reduces neuroinflammation in basal ganglia circuits. Supports neuronal health in PRRT2-expressing cells.
→
Magnesium threonate — crosses blood brain barrier. Nav channel cofactor. Increases CNS magnesium concentration in exactly the circuits affected.
→
Taurine 2,000mg — GABA-A agonist. Directly adds inhibitory tone to compensate for PRRT2 excitability gap.
→
Lacosamide — targets slow inactivation of Nav channels specifically. More precise than carbamazepine. Quiets paroxysmal burst pattern without cognitive suppression.
→
Blood sugar stability — prevents metabolic stress that lowers paroxysmal tipping threshold. Critical on heat-exposure work days.
